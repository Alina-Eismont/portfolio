UX Design Process

Before developing the final website, I followed a structured UX process using Figma for research, planning, and layout development. This process ensured a seamless user experience, an intuitive menu structure, and a visually engaging design that represents the brand identity of California Burrito Co.

  • Research and Planning

    - Conducted competitor analysis of fast-casual restaurant websites to understand best practices in the industry.

    - Designed a site map to define the website’s structure and create an efficient user flow for menu exploration and location search.

    - Focused on mobile-first design, ensuring an optimized experience for users browsing on-the-go.

  • Mood Board and Style Guide

    - Developed a mood board in Figma to capture the brand’s lively, inviting, and flavorful identity.

    - Selected a bold color palette inspired by the Mexican food culture, using warm tones to evoke appetite and excitement.

    - Chose typography that balances a modern and friendly appeal, aligning with the fast-casual dining experience.

  • Wireframing and Prototyping

    - Designed mobile-first wireframes to ensure a seamless experience on smaller screens before scaling up to desktop versions.

    - Focused on a clean, structured layout, placing high-priority content like the menu and contact details above the fold for quick access.

    - Ensured a fast-loading, responsive design that adapts across different screen sizes, maintaining readability and functionality.
